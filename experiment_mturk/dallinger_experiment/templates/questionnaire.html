Experiment debriefing (optional to read)

The goal of this study was to investigate how information is processed, transformed and transmitted in communication. In this experiment, two conditions were studied - transmission chains, as in the children’s game “Telephone” and simple networks (every participant heard multiple stories and passed them to new participants). Depending on the condition, single or multiple initial participants were given a text about superbugs, e.g. antibacterial resistance. After the time for reading the text, participants were asked to rewrite the information they have read. These texts were passed further to the next participant(s) - in the case of the transmission chain, the participant only received a single text (original or from another participant), while in the networked scenario, participants received multiple texts and their texts were passed to other participants.

We were interested in determining how much the text changes in each iteration, but also how participants deal with different texts, e.g. coming from different sources/changed in a different way.

Your participation is not only greatly appreciated by the researchers involved, but the data collected could possibly help us learn how to communicate better information of interest on social networks.

If you have any questions about this study, please do not hesitate to contact Mathew Hardy at mdhardy@princeton.edu.
